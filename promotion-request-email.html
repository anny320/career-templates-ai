Promotion Request Email Template

A short, direct email that opens the promotion conversation with your manager — without sounding like you're issuing an ultimatum. Copy the template below, fill in your own results, and send it before your next review cycle.

The Template

Subject line
Time to talk about my role & next steps
Body
Hi [Manager's name], I'd like to set up time to talk about my growth and a potential move to [target role/level]. Over the past [timeframe], I've taken on [1–2 concrete responsibilities or projects], and the results have included [specific outcome, e.g. "reduced onboarding time by 30%" or "led the migration that cut support tickets in half"]. I'd love to walk through how my role has evolved and talk through what a promotion to [target title] would look like, including what you'd want to see from me between now and the next review cycle. Could we grab 20–30 minutes this week or next? Thanks, [Your name]

How to use this template

01

Time it deliberately. Send this 4–6 weeks before your company's formal review or budget cycle — early enough for your manager to build a case upward, not so early it feels speculative.

02

Replace the bracket with one real number. "Helped the team" is forgettable. "Cut deployment time from 2 days to 4 hours" gets remembered in the room you're not in.

03

Ask for a conversation, not a decision. The email's job is to get the meeting on the calendar. Make your full case there, with examples, not in the email itself.

04

Leave money out of it. Mentioning salary here shifts the conversation from "am I ready" to "what do you want," which is a harder conversation to win.

Common questions

When should I send a promotion request email?

4–6 weeks before your company's review cycle or budget planning period — never the same week you want an answer. Your manager needs runway to advocate for you upward.

Should I mention salary in a promotion request email?

No. Keep the email focused on scope, impact, and readiness for the next level. Save compensation for the actual conversation, once your manager has agreed to discuss the promotion.

How long should this email be?

Under 200 words. It should open a conversation, not make the entire case in writing. Lead with the ask, support it with one or two concrete results, propose a time to talk.

What if my manager doesn't respond?

Follow up once after 5–7 business days with a short, friendly nudge. If there's still silence, raise it directly in your next 1:1 instead of sending a third email.
