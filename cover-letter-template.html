New to cover letters? A cover letter is a short letter (3–4 paragraphs) you send alongside your CV. Its job is simple: tell the employer why you want this job and why you're the right fit — in your own words, not just a list of your qualifications.

Tip: mention something specific about the company — a project, value, or programme you genuinely admire. This is the line that separates your letter from 90% of applicants.

How to write a strong cover letter

01

Address it to a real person if you can. Check LinkedIn or the company website for the hiring manager's name. "Dear Ms. Dlamini" is always stronger than "Dear Hiring Manager."

02

Be specific about why this company. Employers can tell when a letter is generic. One specific detail — a project, a value, a campaign you admire — shows you actually researched them.

03

Use numbers wherever you can. "I helped grow the team" is vague. "I trained 12 new volunteers over 3 months" is memorable. Even small numbers make a claim feel real.

04

Keep it to one page. Three to four short paragraphs is ideal. If it takes more than 30 seconds to read, it's too long.

05

Proofread out loud. Read your letter out loud before sending. If you stumble over a sentence, rewrite it. Errors in a cover letter signal carelessness — take the extra five minutes.

Common questions

Do I always need a cover letter?

Not always — some online applications don't ask for one. But if there's an option to include one, always do. It's an extra chance to stand out that most applicants skip.

What's the difference between "Yours sincerely" and "Yours faithfully"?

Use "Yours sincerely" when you've addressed the letter to a named person (Dear Ms. Dlamini). Use "Yours faithfully" when you've used a generic opening (Dear Hiring Manager / Dear Sir or Madam). This is standard professional English — getting it wrong is a small thing that some employers notice.

Can I use the same cover letter for every job?

No. You should have a base template (which is what this is), but the "why this company" paragraph must be rewritten for every application. Generic letters get ignored — a specific, tailored one takes 10 extra minutes and makes a real difference.

I don't have much experience — what do I put?

Use what you have: volunteer work, school projects, community involvement, sports teams you've led, events you've organised. Transferable skills count. What matters is showing you can do the work, not that you've had a formal job doing it.

How long should a cover letter be?

3–4 paragraphs, fitting on one page. Roughly 250–350 words. Short enough to read in under a minute, long enough to make your case.
